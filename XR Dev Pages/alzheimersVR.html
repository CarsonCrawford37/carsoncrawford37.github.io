Virtual Reality

Preface

Alzheimer's is a very difficult disease to catch early on, due to how similar they are to the symptoms of normal aging. However, recent studies have identified a correlation between a diminishing sense of smell and Alzheimer's. In response to this need for early detection tools, a Vertically Integrated Project (VIP) class at Boise State University took on the challenge. Leveraging the immersive nature of virtual reality technology and a custom-built scent device, BrainHealthVR designed a cost-effective testing simulation to aid in the pre-diagnosis of this degenerative disease that regular doctors can use with ease.

Contributions

As a summer intern, my primary responsibility was to complete the finalization of the simulation for presentation to a partner company. This company had agreed to conduct initial tests of the program with patients. My role involved ensuring that the simulation was thoroughly prepared and ready for testing, meeting the rigorous standards necessary for this crucial phase of development.

This meant ensuring the scent device worked flawlessly with the program, creating smooth user interactions, and coordinating with engineering team and the client to make the product right. Another part of my role was creating a tutorial phase for the user to help get them acquainted with the VR space. This was to help reduce motion sickness and confusion when entering the actual simulation as well as increase accessibility for individuals who have never used VR before either.
