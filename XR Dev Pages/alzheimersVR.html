Alzheimer's Pre-Diagnostic Simulation

Role: VR Programmer
Engine: Unity 3D
Team Size: 3

About

Alzheimer's is a very difficult disease to catch early on, due to how similar they are to the symptoms of normal aging. However, recent studies have identified a correlation between a diminishing sense of smell and Alzheimer's. In response to this need for early detection tools, a Vertically Integrated Project (VIP) class at Boise State University took on the challenge. Leveraging the immersive nature of virtual reality technology and a custom-built scent device, BrainHealthVR designed a cost-effective testing simulation to aid in the pre-diagnosis of this degenerative disease that regular doctors can use with ease.

As a summer intern, I collaborated with a team of three to finalize the simulation for presentation to a partner company. This company had agreed to conduct initial tests of the program with their patients. My responsibility included ensuring the simulation was thoroughly prepared and ready for testing. This involved enhancing user interactions, optimizing communication between the scent device and the program, and coordinating closely with the engineering team and client to deliver a polished product.

Contributions



In collaboration with the group lead, I also put together an easy to read score based on the data being collected (gaze correctness, time looking at right object, etc.). With intention of this test to be repeated to see any sort of trends the user has between their score sets, this was a quick and easy way for both the user and test proctor can see how they did.

Game and System Design - Being the only programmer at the time, my key responsibilities was ensuring a smooth user interaction and making the diagnostic effective. I started by creating a tutorial specifically designed for individuals who may be unfamiliar with VR technology. This helped mitigate the risk of unintended consequences such as cybersickness or data inaccuracies.
